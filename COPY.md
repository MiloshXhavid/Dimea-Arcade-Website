## Manifesto (Vision section)

Most musicians spend years learning music theory just to play a chord. We think that's backwards.

Harmony shouldn't be a gate you pass through after decades of practice. It should be a playground — something you feel, explore, and own from the moment you touch it.

**Dimea Arcade** builds instruments that give everyone direct, physical access to harmony. Move a joystick. Change a scale. Play something beautiful. Whether you've been playing for thirty years or thirty minutes.

No chord packs. No presets someone else made. Just you, a controller, and infinite harmonic space.

## Founder Story (About section)

**Dimitri Dähler** is a jazz trumpet player, producer, and sound engineer trained at the Hochschule Luzern. Raised in a household of classical musicians, he grew up between jazz and hip hop — performing, producing, searching for new sounds.

The origin of Dimea Arcade was a single frustration: "I wanted chords to improvise over on my trumpet. People told me modular can't do chords. So I built one that could."

That Eurorack rig — an XY joystick at the centre, patched through VCOs, filters and envelopes — became the instrument he brought to his masterproject performance. Slow chord textures. Live trumpet over self-generated harmony. All improvised. The audience called it mesmerizing.

*"I was at exactly the place I'd been searching for years."*

From that moment came a question: why should this only be possible for someone willing to spend years building a modular? Why can't anyone — producer, performer, beginner — have this?

The plugin is the first answer. The hardware is the next. The mission stays the same: **remove the barrier between people and harmony.**

## Hardware Origin (Hardware section — "Where this comes from")

This didn't start as a product. It started as a problem.

A table full of Eurorack modules, an XY joystick, and a trumpet. It worked — beautifully — but it was never something anyone else could just pick up and play.

**DIMEOLA** is the distillation of that idea into one object you can hold in your hands. Two joysticks. Pressure-sensitive pads. Back triggers. Internal chord engine. No computer required. No setup. No patch cables.

Inspired by the Buchla philosophy of rethinking how humans interact with electronic instruments — not keyboards, not knobs, but *space* and *gesture* as musical input.

We're building it now. Be part of it from the start.
