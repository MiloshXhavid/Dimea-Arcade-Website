XY Spatial Harmony Pad
The core performance surface. Y axis walks scale degrees — root and third change pitch. X axis sweeps chord colour — fifth and tension shift interval quality. Every position is harmonically valid. Nothing wrong is possible.
🎵
4-Voice MIDI Architecture
Root, Third, Fifth, and Tension — four independent voices, each on its own MIDI channel. Per-voice interval offsets, octave settings, and trigger sources. Route to separate instruments or blend into one.
🎼
Scale & Key System
30+ scale presets — Major, Minor, all modes, pentatonics, and more. Full custom scale builder for non-standard tunings. Transpose on the fly. Smart chord display names every voicing in real time.
Smart Chord Display
🔁
4-Lane Chord Looper
Four independent record lanes: REC captures full MIDI, GATES records rhythm only (joystick steers pitch during playback), JOY captures continuous XY movement as a replayable curve. DAW-synced or free-running.
DAW Sync
🥁
8-Step Arpeggiator
Pattern grid with ON, TIE, and OFF states per step. Adjustable rate (synced or free), order (Up / Down / Random / Chord), and gate percentage. Stack with the looper for layered rhythmic movement.
🌊
LFO Modulation Engine
Multiple waveform shapes with DAW sync or free rate. Routes to any CC destination — filter cutoff, resonance, voice parameters, and custom targets. Sister cross-modulation between LFO pairs. REC mode captures joystick movement as a custom LFO shape.
8 CC Destinations
👆
TouchPlates & Random Trigger
Per-voice trigger sources: PAD fires on click, JOY re-triggers continuously on position change, RND fires at random intervals via the built-in random trigger engine. Mix sources per voice for polyrhythmic textures.
🕹
Gamepad Control
Connect any PS4/PS5 or compatible gamepad. Left stick for pitch, right stick for filter/mod. Shoulder buttons control the looper. Face buttons change scale. Full button mapping — perform without touching the mouse.

02About
About
Harmony shouldn't live behind a theory wall. It should be something you feel from the first note.

Harmony shouldn't be a gate you pass through after decades of practice. It should be a playground — something you feel, explore, and own from the moment you touch it.

Dimea Arcade builds instruments that give everyone direct, physical access to harmony. Move a joystick. Change a scale. Play something beautiful. Whether you've been playing for thirty years or thirty minutes.

Dimitri Dähler is a jazz trumpet player, producer, and sound engineer trained at the Hochschule Luzern. Raised in a household of classical musicians, he grew up between jazz and hip hop — performing, producing, searching for new sounds.

The origin of Dimea Arcade was a single challenge: "I wanted chords to improvise over on my trumpet. People told me modular can't do chords. So I built one that could."

That Eurorack rig — an XY joystick at the centre, patched through VCOs, filters and envelopes — became the instrument he brought to his masterproject performance. Slow chord textures. Live trumpet over self-generated harmony. All improvised. The audience called it mesmerizing.

From that moment came a question: why should this only be possible for someone willing to spend years building a modular? Why can't anyone — producer, performer, beginner — have this? The plugin is the first answer. The hardware is the next.

"

I was at exactly the place I'd been searching for years.

— Dimitri Dähler

The Vision

Harmony shouldn't live inside a laptop.

Every musician knows the feeling: you have an idea, but before you can play it you're opening a DAW, selecting a plugin, clicking through presets, drawing MIDI notes. By the time the computer is ready, the moment is gone.

DIMEOLA is the opposite of that. It's a chord synthesizer you hold in your hands — no screen, no menus, no setup. Two joysticks map your fingers directly to harmony. Press a button, change the scale. Move left, move right — the music follows.

The goal isn't to build another MIDI controller. It's to make harmonic improvisation as immediate and physical as playing a guitar. Bring it on stage. Clip it to your belt. Play.

01
Zero Latency Thinking
No screen between you and the sound. Every control is tactile and immediate — scale changes, octave shifts, voice triggers. The cognitive load drops to zero so you can focus on music.
02
Harmony for Everyone
The scale engine ensures every position on the joystick is a valid chord. You can't play a wrong note — but you can play anything from a simple triad to a dense 7th voicing, just by where you move.
03
Built to Perform
Gamepad form factor means two-thumb control while standing, walking, or moving. Back triggers for velocity. The looper lets you layer phrases without stopping. One device, full band-in-a-box capability.
04
Open Architecture
Daisy Seed runs Pure Data compiled via hvcc — completely open and flashable. MIDI in/out means it plugs into any setup: modular, hardware synth, or as a controller for the plugin. No walled garden.
"
At the masterproject, I had a Eurorack rig running live chords under my trumpet — XY joystick, self-patched, nothing pre-programmed. The feeling was exactly right. The problem: the rig was completely mine. Years of patches, my logic, my hands. Hand it to someone else and they'd be lost. DIMEOLA is the same idea in a form anyone can play.
— Dimitri Dähler, HSLU Musik Luzern · Masterproject 2026
🕹
Two Joysticks
Left joystick: pitch plane — Y axis moves Root + 3rd, X axis moves 5th + 7th across the scale. Right joystick: filter cutoff, resonance, or any assignable parameter. Independent, simultaneous.
✋
4 Pressure-Sensitive Touchplates
Root · 3rd · 5th · Tension — each plate pressure-sensitive for individual volume control per voice. Light touch for ghost notes, push harder for full expression.
🎚
4× 3-Way Trigger Switches
Per-voice trigger mode selection: PAD (touchplate triggers), JOY (joystick position triggers), or RND (random trigger). Flip between modes mid-performance without stopping.
🔁
Per-Layer Internal Looper
Pitch gestures, gate patterns, and modulation movements can be looped independently. Sync via MIDI clock in or internal clock. Loop subdivisions: 1/4, 1/8, 1/16, 3/4.
🎼
Scale Engine + Octave/Degree Switching
12 LED scale select buttons — preset scales plus custom scale programming. Octave switches and scale degree switches (like the plugin) for instant key changes on the fly.
🔌
I/O & Connectivity
MIDI in + out, clock/CV in, stereo audio out (internal synth engine). USB-C for charging, firmware updates, and standalone software. Swappable battery — play anywhere, indefinitely.
🎮
Gamepad Form Factor
PS5-sized body. Back triggers on the underside. No screen, no menus, no diving. One alternate-function switch for secondary controls. Pick it up and play in under 10 seconds.
⚡
Standalone Synth Engine
4-voice internal synthesis on Daisy Seed — no computer needed. Runs the Pure Data patch compiled via hvcc. Headphones in, play. Or send MIDI out to any external synth or modular.

DIMEA App.

the instrument.

IN DEVELOPMENT — iOS · Android · macOS

No hardware yet? No problem. The DIMEA app turns any iOS or Android touchscreen into a fully functional DIMEOLA controller — with the same scale engine, XY pad, and looper from the plugin.

When you do get the hardware, the app becomes its brain — a visual editor for scales, presets, firmware updates, and routing.

Mode 1
Standalone Instrument
XY pad on screen. Built-in synth engine. Plug in headphones, play. No computer, no DAW, no setup.
Mode 2
MIDI Controller
Routes 4-voice MIDI to any external synth, modular, or DAW via USB-C or BLE MIDI. Same chord logic as the plugin.
Mode 3
DIMEOLA Companion
Visual scale editor, preset manager, trigger mode config, and firmware updater for the MK1 hardware. The screen the hardware doesn't have.
